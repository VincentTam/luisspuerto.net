    The variable that holds this setting is hibernatemode and 0 is simple sleep, 3 is both and 25 is just safe sleep. In essence what is going on here is:

    • Simple Sleep ‚Äî `hibernatemode 0`: Your computer keeps all the info in the RAM and stops all the rest of the computer. The RAM still has power and if you happen to run our of battery ‚Äîafter quite long time‚Äî you lose everything ‚Äînot really, but let‚Äôs be catastrophical and think in the worse picture. However, you usually you don‚Äôt let your Mac sleeping and unplugged for more than a week, do you? I think the battery in this state can last more than a week, let be conservative here. This mode is the quickest, and usually your Mac is sleeping just after a couple of seconds you close the lid.
    • Both ‚Äî hibenatemode 3: This is the standard mode and how most of the MacBooks behave. When you close the lid the Mac just normally sleep, but before it‚Äôs going to save its state in the sleepimage.     • Safe Sleep ‚Äî hibernatemode 25: This is the hibernation mode in windows, in other words, the computer saves everything to the sleepimage and them disconnect power from everything. This mode is also really safe, perhaps safer than the previous one for some people and cases, but it‚Äôs the slowest. Usually the sleep process is the same than in the previous one, but it‚Äôs always going to wake up from the sleepimage, regardless of it has run out of battery or not. So it‚Äôs going to take more or less time depending of the capacity of your machine to read the sleepimage.

    There is more info about the sleep modes here.
